Chad & Kim Wagner

The story behind the pictures...
Chad & Kim left for vacation on Saturday, January 27, 2001 to visit their friends Ron & Cindy (and their new baby girl Jordan!) in Seattle, Washington. Having access to the airways, Ron & Cindy planned out a day of gambling in Las Vegas. Never letting a good opportunity go to waste, Chad grabbed a wedding band, grabbed Kim and jumped on the plane with Ron, Cindy & little three week-old Jordan. Hours later, the whole crew was busy flushing their $$$ down some slots amidst the shouts of
"WHAT KIND OF PARENTS WOULD BRING THEIR BABY TO A SMOKY CASINO?!"
So as Kim was busy feeding Caesar dollars into a machine, little did she realize that the real jackpot was at the bottom of her gambling cup. One by one she pulled coins out, until finally she dug out what she thought was a bottle cap (?!). She stared blankly at it for quite a while until it all started to make sense - aided, of course, by her boyfriend down on one knee and her girl friend crying in the corner.

We immediately left Caesar's, both to find a chapel and also because we were kicked out by the casino Barney Fife for having a baby on the gambling floor.

"THERE GOES THAT BABY AGAIN!"

In the true Las Vegas tradition, nothing short of Elvis would do, and the yellow pages offered up exactly what we were looking for...

Ritchie - the driver and our host for the evening - picked us up in a dark blue Caddy limo (white trash footnote: It had a TV and a bar!). A quick stop at the County Court House and we would be headed to the chapel, but the court house deserves a mention.

First, they have an express window for wedding licenses.
Second, you don't need anything - no ID, no records, nothing (they even provide the little pencils).

In and out in minutes - during which Ritchie ran Ron, Cindy and the baby on a quick tour of the back streets of Vegas.

OFF TO THE CHAPEL!
Obviously, anything this spontaneous is bound to introduce last minutes doubts, but all fears were quickly dispelled once we learned that both Jon Bon Jovi and Lorenzo Lamas were married here. All at once everything was right in the world again.
We spared no expense during our wedding, opting for everything that we could get to make it special. That included such finery as pens, postcards, bumper stickers, and even key chains! Since Elvis was invited, we even received a complimentary reproduction of the King's marriage certificate with a picture of Elvis himself superimposed on the front.
The chapel was a beautiful little hall with pews and stained glass galore. Our service was going to be performed at midnight by Reverend Gutierrez, and all we had left to wait for was the grand entrance of Elvis Presley himself.

Elvis arrived, and the ceremony began. To the tune of Love Me Tender, Kim was escorted down the aisle.


Reverend Gutierrez:
"And who gives this bride to be wed?"

Elvis:
"On behalf of her family and friends, the King of Rock & Roll, sir."

The ceremony was incredible. Reverend Gutierrez gave a very powerful service, Ron and Cindy stood up for us, and little baby Jordon served as the youngest flower girl ever in the history of the Graceland Wedding Chapel.

And she lost her sock.
Just hours after seeing it for the first time, Kim received the wedding band signifying marriage. Chad, in haste, didn't come prepared with a ring for himself, so the Best Man's wedding band served for the symbolic purpose.

There were tears, laughter (a lot of laughter), an occasional baby gurgle, and even a Unity Candle. And during the entire ceremony the room was filled with the soft strumming of an approving Elvis.

Once the vows had been exchanged and the ceremony brought to a close, Elvis serenaded the new couple with classics from his movies. The couple was invited to join in and the whole thing wound up with the trio arm-in-arm high-kicking down the aisle to a resounding version of Viva Las Vegas!
A few shots for posterity, and the couple was out the door.
